Taipei Medical University

Collaboration agreements have been signed with the Ministry of Health in Eswatini and the Nigerian National Institute for Medical Research. Since 2009, Taipei Medical University has stationed a medical team in Eswatini. In addition to providing clinical services to the people of Eswatini, the team organizes periodic medical missions to remote rural communities in the country. The collaboration between Taipei Medical University and universities and healthcare institutions in Africa, led by the Department of Medical Parasitology and Tropical Diseases, has yielded fruitful results. From 2009 to 2020, Professor Fan Chia-Kwung from the School of Medicine led the Parasitic Disease Prevention and Control Team and the Flying Yang International Volunteer Team on epidemiological investigations and service learning in Eswatini. During the research, collaboration with local technical and public health personnel was established, resulting in frequent exchanges and significant achievements, earning a good reputation from the Ministry of Health in Eswatini.

Furthermore, from 2010 to 2016, Taipei Medical University stationed a medical team in the Democratic Republic of São Tomé and Príncipe in West Africa. Before October each year, Professor Fan Chia-Kwung led the Parasitic Disease Prevention and Control Team

In 2011, a delegation of Nigerian senators visited Taiwan and learned about Taipei Medical University's parasitic disease prevention and control projects in Eswatini and São Tomé and Príncipe. The Deputy Director of the Nigerian National Institute for Medical Research, Prof. Akinwale, expressed interest in collaboration and reached a consensus on assisting their researchers in training and development of high-level talents. Mr. Gyang, a staff member from the institute, was admitted to Taipei Medical University's doctoral program in 2012 and obtained his Ph.D. degree in 2016. He has since returned to the Nigerian National Institute for Medical Research and continues to serve there.

During the global COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, Taipei Medical University responded to a request from the Ministry of Health in Eswatini by organizing the "COVID-19 Prevention Lecture and Training Seminar" for the graduates of the 2020 Bachelor of Post-Baccalaureate Medicine program from I-Shou University. The seminar featured experts from the "Taiwan We Go Team," who shared their experiences in respiratory management, introduction of respiratory equipment, and personal protective equipment (PPE) usage, strengthening the understanding of COVID-19 among medical students from Eswatini and enabling them to contribute immediately upon their return to their home country to combat the pandemic.
