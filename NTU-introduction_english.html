National Taiwan University

National Taiwan University

National Taiwan University have partnerships with three affiliated schools in South Africa. These include:

1. University of Pretoria: We have one collaborative project ongoing with this institution.
2. Stellenbosch University: We have two collaborative projects ongoing with this institution.
3. University of Cape Town: We have one collaborative project ongoing with this institution.

In addition, interns from the College of Public Health at National Taiwan University have traveled all the way to Malawi in West Africa to participate in international cooperation efforts. They assist local residents in combating threats from diseases such as HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and cholera. Despite the challenges faced in Malawi, including inadequate infrastructure and frequent power outages and water supply issues, the interns from the College of Public Health at National Taiwan University remain enthusiastic and eager to contribute their expertise.

Since 2011 to 2019, a total of 19 students from the Department of Public Health at National Taiwan University have traveled to Malawi for internships. Collaborating with Taiwan BIC Charity Foundation, Pingtung Christian Hospital, and Norwegian International Aid, they engage in community-based work, including epidemiological investigations, data analysis, epidemic prevention assistance, and evaluation of preventive measures. They also conduct various public health promotion activities, such as educating school-age girls on proper use of sanitary pads and emphasizing the importance of menstrual hygiene.

After completing their internships in Malawi, many of these interns have continued their careers at organizations such as the World Health Organization or non-governmental organizations, contributing to global health initiatives.

Furthermore, the College of Public Health at National Taiwan University has led a Taiwan team in assisting the island nation of São Tomé and Príncipe in Central and Western Africa in the fight against malaria. They have taught local residents various tasks, including medication distribution, spraying, administration, blood sampling, blood testing, case registration, and conducting questionnaire surveys. As a result, the prevalence of malaria in the country has been reduced from 50% to 1%, earning recognition from the African Leaders Malaria Alliance.

Achievements

  • Research focus: National Taiwan University has conducted research on various tropical diseases, including malaria, schistosomiasis, and neglected tropical diseases.
  • Epidemiological studies: Researchers have conducted epidemiological studies to understand disease transmission patterns and risk factors in African settings.
  • Diagnostic methods: National Taiwan University has developed diagnostic methods to improve the detection and diagnosis of tropical diseases in African populations.
  • Treatment strategies: The university has explored and developed treatment strategies for tropical diseases, aiming to improve patient outcomes in African countries.
  • Capacity building: National Taiwan University has contributed to capacity building by training local healthcare professionals in Africa, enhancing their skills and knowledge in tropical medicine.
  • Community-based healthcare: The university has implemented community-based healthcare projects, including mobile clinics, health screening campaigns, and community health promotion programs, to improve healthcare access and address specific healthcare challenges in African communities.
  • Collaboration: National Taiwan University has collaborated with local institutions, organizations, and governments in Africa to conduct research, share expertise, and implement healthcare initiatives.
